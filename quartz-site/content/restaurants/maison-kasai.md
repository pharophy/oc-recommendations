## Description

Maison Kasai turns teppanyaki into a polished performance, with French-Japanese menus, rooftop energy, chef choreography, and a strong "show is the point" presentation style.

## What Makes It Unique

It lands in the same lane as Pan Am in that the meal is inseparable from the staging. The grills, chef performance, and Level 8 atmosphere make it feel like a hospitality production, not a standard special-occasion dinner.

## Notes

- Reservations: OpenTable and the official site both support booking.
- Dress code: The official site lists a dress code page; OpenTable describes the venue as business casual.
- Age policy: The official site says the restaurant is adults-only after 9:00 p.m., so I marked this `Mixed / Time-dependent`.
- Other: The official menu currently lists a teppanyaki dinner starting at `$110 per person`, which is why this sits above an ordinary steakhouse price point even before drinks.
