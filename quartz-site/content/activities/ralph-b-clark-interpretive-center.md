# Ralph B. Clark Interpretive Center

The Ralph B. Clark Interpretive Center is one of the best local answers to the "La Brea Tar Pits energy, but in Orange County" category. It focuses on prehistoric Orange County through fossils, exhibits, and hands-on education programming.

## Why It Stands Out

This is especially strong if your child likes fossils, rocks, and discovery-based science. It is not just a playground with a theme; it is a real paleontology-centered stop tied to the region's natural history.

## Practical Notes

- OC Parks says the center includes marine fossils up to 80 million years old and more recent terrestrial mammals.
- The center is open Wednesday through Saturday from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m.
- It sits inside Clark Regional Park, which also hosts kid-focused programs tied to fossils and geology.
- Parking fees may apply at the park.
