# New Car Buying Research Pack

Last updated: March 26, 2026

This folder is focused on three jobs:

1. Find the closest thing to dealer invoice and a realistic target price.
2. Find current rebates, APR offers, lease offers, loyalty/conquest cash, and member-program discounts.
3. Negotiate or pre-negotiate without relying on a salesperson's framing.

## Bottom line

If you only want the shortest high-confidence stack, use:

- `Edmunds` for invoice visibility plus a market-based target price.
- `Kelley Blue Book (KBB)` for a second market-price check.
- `TrueCar` for local transaction-based price benchmarking and dealer quotes.
- `CarsDirect` for fast-moving rebate / APR / lease intelligence.
- `Costco Auto Program` or `AAA Auto Buying` if you want a cleaner pre-negotiated path.
- `CarEdge` if you want extra negotiation guidance and invoice-oriented tooling in one place.

## Best tools by job

### 1. Best tool for invoice-price lookup

`Best overall: Edmunds`

Why:

- Edmunds explicitly shows invoice pricing in its Build & Price flow for many brands and pairs it with its market-based price guidance.
- Edmunds also states that invoice is not true dealer net cost because holdback and dealer cash can sit behind it. That is exactly the right way to use invoice: as a reference point, not a final target.

Use it for:

- Building your exact trim and options.
- Seeing invoice plus a realistic transaction-based comparison.
- Avoiding the common mistake of treating invoice as the dealer's real bottom line.

Limits:

- Some brands may not show invoice.
- Invoice alone is not enough for negotiation.

Key sources:

- Edmunds invoice help: https://help.edmunds.com/hc/en-us/articles/206102377-What-is-dealer-invoice-or-dealer-cost
- Edmunds how to find invoice: https://help.edmunds.com/hc/en-us/articles/360020890413-How-to-find-TMV-Invoice-Pricing-on-Edmunds
- Edmunds pricing basics: https://www.edmunds.com/car-buying/pricing-basics-for-new-car-buying.html

`Best second source: CarEdge`

Why:

- CarEdge offers a free dealer-invoice lookup flow and packages it with target discount guidance, negotiation guidance, and a checklist.
- This makes it useful as a practical negotiation assistant, especially if you want a single "buying toolkit" instead of jumping between sites.

Use it for:

- Quick invoice estimate.
- Target-discount guidance.
- Negotiation prompts and process support.

Limits:

- It is more sales-funnel-driven than Edmunds.
- Their own site notes pricing and incentives are dynamic and subject to change.

Key source:

- CarEdge dealer invoice tool: https://caredge.com/dealer-invoice

`Best market-price cross-check: KBB`

Why:

- KBB's strongest value is not invoice itself. It is Fair Purchase Price and Fair Market Range, updated at least weekly and regionalized.
- That makes KBB one of the best sanity checks after you look at invoice elsewhere.

Use it for:

- Verifying whether your target price is actually realistic in your region this week.
- Checking whether a dealer's "discount" is really just a return to market.

Limits:

- KBB itself says invoice is only a reference point and may not resemble actual dealer cost.

Key sources:

- KBB new cars hub: https://www.kbb.com/new-cars/
- KBB new-car FAQ: https://www.kbb.com/faq/new-cars/

## Best tools for current deals and incentives

### Best overall current-deals tracker: CarsDirect

Why:

- CarsDirect is the strongest single place I found for current purchase rebates, 0% APR deals, lease deals, EV deals, and brand/model-specific summaries.
- Its March 2026 pages are recent and continuously updated.
- It often calls out hidden or stackable discounts that shoppers can miss.

Use it for:

- Finding which models are genuinely deal-heavy this month.
- Checking whether APR or cash rebate is the better move.
- Spotting member or conquest bonuses that are easy to miss.

Limits:

- It is still an aggregator, so verify final eligibility with the manufacturer or dealer.

Key sources:

- Best rebates and incentives: https://www.carsdirect.com/deals-articles/best-new-car-deals
- Best 0% APR deals: https://www.carsdirect.com/deals-articles/best-zero-percent-financing-deals

### Best broad consumer incentive hub: Edmunds

Why:

- Edmunds has a simple incentives page and clearly warns that incentives vary by region and month.
- Good for a quick sweep before you talk numbers.

Use it for:

- A fast check on whether your chosen model has meaningful national or regional support.

Key source:

- Edmunds incentives: https://www.edmunds.com/car-incentives/

### Best transaction-and-incentive combo: TrueCar

Why:

- TrueCar combines market-price context with current incentives.
- TrueCar says pricing is updated weekly and incentives are checked daily, which is strong for a moving target.

Use it for:

- Local price benchmarking.
- Dealer quote collection.
- Incentive visibility while comparing actual offers.

Key sources:

- TrueCar FAQ: https://www.truecar.com/faq/
- TrueCar incentives guide: https://www.truecar.com/blog/your-guide-to-car-buying-incentives/

### Best consumer-friendly explanation of financing tradeoffs: Cars.com

Why:

- Cars.com's current 0% APR coverage is useful because it highlights the real issue: financing offers and cash incentives are sometimes mutually exclusive.

Use it for:

- Deciding between rebate-heavy offers and subsidized APR.

## Best tools for negotiation or pre-negotiation

### Best low-hassle pre-negotiation path: Costco Auto Program

Why:

- Costco offers prearranged pricing through approved dealers.
- The site explicitly says you can stack manufacturer incentives on top of Costco pricing where available.
- This is one of the cleanest options if you want less back-and-forth.

Best for:

- Buyers who value speed and lower friction more than squeezing the absolute final few hundred dollars.
- People who already have Costco membership.

Key source:

- Costco Auto Program: https://www.costcoauto.com/

### Best membership alternative: AAA Auto Buying

Why:

- AAA's auto buying program is powered by TrueCar and emphasizes upfront savings and price comparison.
- Good if you already have AAA and want a lighter-touch version of the TrueCar flow.

Best for:

- Members who want pre-negotiated structure without building their own process from scratch.

Key source:

- AAA Auto Buying Resources: https://mwg.aaa.com/automotive/auto-buying-resources

### Best DIY negotiation support: CarEdge

Why:

- CarEdge is more negotiation-forward than the mainstream pricing sites.
- It explicitly packages invoice, target discount, negotiation guide, and checklists together.

Best for:

- Buyers who want scripts and leverage, not just price data.

Key source:

- CarEdge dealer invoice tool: https://caredge.com/dealer-invoice

### Best dealer-competition workflow: TrueCar

Why:

- TrueCar's biggest advantage is turning pricing research into dealer responses.
- Its price graph and local transaction data help you tell whether an offer is actually competitive in your market.

Best for:

- Email / phone pre-negotiation.

## Best stack depending on your style

### Cheapest and mostly free

1. Build your exact car on Edmunds.
2. Check KBB Fair Purchase Price.
3. Check TrueCar local pricing.
4. Check CarsDirect for rebates / APR / lease offers.
5. Email 4 to 8 dealers with an out-the-door request.

### Lowest-stress member route

1. Check Costco Auto Program.
2. Check AAA if you are a member there instead.
3. Still cross-check with Edmunds or KBB so you know whether the "prearranged" number is merely decent or actually strong.

### Most aggressive negotiation route

1. Pull invoice and market data from Edmunds plus KBB.
2. Pull current incentives from CarsDirect, Edmunds, and TrueCar.
3. Use CarEdge negotiation guidance.
4. Make dealers compete over email or text using out-the-door pricing only.

## What matters more than invoice

Invoice is useful, but these matter more:

- Actual recent local transaction prices.
- Manufacturer-to-customer incentives.
- Dealer cash or hidden support that may not be obvious in ads.
- Inventory age and whether the car is sitting.
- Whether rebate and special APR can be stacked.
- Out-the-door price, not just selling price.

In practical terms: a car priced `below invoice` is not automatically a great deal, and a car priced `above invoice` is not automatically bad. The right comparison is your local market value after current incentives and before finance-office add-ons.

## Recommended workflow

1. Choose the exact trim and options.
2. Pull invoice / pricing benchmarks from Edmunds and KBB.
3. Pull market-value / local-dealer context from TrueCar.
4. Pull current rebate, APR, lease, loyalty, conquest, student, military, and member-program offers from CarsDirect, Edmunds, and TrueCar.
5. Check Costco or AAA if you qualify.
6. Ask dealers for a written out-the-door quote with:
   - selling price before incentives
   - each incentive listed separately
   - dealer fees
   - add-ons
   - tax, title, registration
7. Compare complete out-the-door numbers, not monthly payment.
8. Only discuss financing after the selling price is locked.
