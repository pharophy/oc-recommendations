# Sources And Research Notes

Research date: March 26, 2026

## Primary sources used

### Edmunds

- Edmunds explains that invoice price is useful but usually higher than the dealer's real net cost because of holdback and dealer cash, and that invoice excludes some charges and consumer rebates:
  https://help.edmunds.com/hc/en-us/articles/206102377-What-is-dealer-invoice-or-dealer-cost
- Edmunds documents where to find invoice and market-value pricing in its Build & Price flow:
  https://help.edmunds.com/hc/en-us/articles/360020890413-How-to-find-TMV-Invoice-Pricing-on-Edmunds
- Edmunds' current incentives page says incentives vary by region and month:
  https://www.edmunds.com/car-incentives/

### Kelley Blue Book

- KBB says Fair Purchase Price is based on actual transactions, reviewed weekly, and regionalized:
  https://www.kbb.com/faq/new-cars/
- KBB says Fair Market Range is based on what people can reasonably expect to pay this week in their area:
  https://www.kbb.com/faq/new-cars/
- KBB also says invoice is only a reference point and may not resemble actual dealer cost:
  https://www.kbb.com/faq/new-cars/
- KBB new-cars pricing hub states its Fair Market Range / Fair Purchase Price is updated weekly:
  https://www.kbb.com/new-cars/

### TrueCar

- TrueCar says pricing is updated weekly and incentives are checked daily:
  https://www.truecar.com/faq/
- TrueCar says its pricing typically uses recent transactions, usually from the last four weeks:
  https://www.truecar.com/faq/
- TrueCar explains that "fair" depends on local market value and uses a Price Graph:
  https://www.truecar.com/blog/getting-a-fair-price-on-your-next-vehicle/
- TrueCar's incentives guide lays out incentive types including cash, dealer cash, loyalty, trade-in, conquest, and EV incentives:
  https://www.truecar.com/blog/your-guide-to-car-buying-incentives/

### CarEdge

- CarEdge's dealer-invoice page says its toolkit includes invoice price, target discount, negotiation guide, exclusive data, and a car-buying checklist:
  https://caredge.com/dealer-invoice
- CarEdge also notes vehicle pricing and incentives are dynamic and subject to change:
  https://caredge.com/dealer-invoice

### Costco Auto Program

- Costco Auto Program advertises prearranged pricing through approved dealers:
  https://www.costcoauto.com/
- Costco also states manufacturer incentives can be combined with its prearranged pricing where available:
  https://www.costcoauto.com/

### AAA Auto Buying

- AAA says its auto-buying program is powered by TrueCar and helps compare prices with upfront savings:
  https://mwg.aaa.com/automotive/auto-buying-resources

### CarsDirect

- CarsDirect's March 2026 rebates page says it reviewed thousands of rebates and updates continuously:
  https://www.carsdirect.com/deals-articles/best-new-car-deals
- CarsDirect's March 2026 0% APR page says it updates continuously as new offers are announced:
  https://www.carsdirect.com/deals-articles/best-zero-percent-financing-deals

### Cars.com

- Cars.com's March 2026 0% APR roundup notes that 0% financing can save thousands, but those offers can be mutually exclusive with other incentives:
  https://www.cars.com/articles/every-new-car-offering-0-financing-522196/

## Research conclusions

### Best invoice source

`Edmunds` is the best first stop because it pairs invoice with a transaction-based pricing view and is explicit about invoice's limits.

### Best market reality check

`KBB` and `TrueCar` are the best cross-checks for what people actually pay in your area.

### Best incentive tracker

`CarsDirect` is the best current-deals tracker I found for quickly scanning which models have unusual rebates, APR offers, lease support, or stackable bonuses right now.

### Best pre-negotiation path

`Costco Auto Program` and `AAA Auto Buying` are the best low-friction options when you prefer a cleaner prearranged-price path.

### Best negotiation-support tool

`CarEdge` is the most negotiation-oriented tool in the group because it packages invoice data, target discount guidance, and scripts/checklists together.

## Important caveats

- Incentives are highly time-sensitive and region-sensitive.
- Invoice is not true dealer net cost.
- MSRP discounts can be misleading when conditional incentives are mixed in.
- 0% APR and cash rebates are often not stackable.
- A written out-the-door quote is more important than any single website number.
