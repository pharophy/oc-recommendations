# Tesla Model 3

Research date: March 26, 2026

## Model year used

`2026`

## Verified pricing

- `MSRP`: Edmunds shows the 2026 Tesla Model 3 Standard at about `$36,990` excluding destination and about `$38,380` in trim pricing context.
- `Invoice price`: `iSeeCars` lists the 2026 Tesla Model 3 invoice as `0` across trims. In practical terms, Tesla is direct-sale, so traditional dealer invoice pricing is not meaningful the way it is for franchised dealers.
- `92780 pricing anchor`: Tesla's direct order flow remains the relevant local pricing source rather than dealer inventory.

## 92780 local inventory and incentives

- Tesla's incentives page says the federal EV tax incentive ended on `September 30, 2025`.
- Tesla's current public incentives support page also says Tesla still uses:
  - inventory-focused offers
  - payment / lease promotions
  - location-based state or utility incentives
- Edmunds surfaced Tesla's `2,000 free Supercharging miles` trade-assistance offer on current Model 3 deals coverage, but Tesla-style savings still depend more on inventory pricing than classic dealer negotiation.

## Buying read

- Tesla is the outlier here because the classic invoice-and-negotiate framework barely applies.
- Your best savings path is usually:
  - watching Tesla inventory pricing
  - checking lease / finance promos
  - checking state or utility incentives
  - using a qualifying trade if Tesla is running a Supercharging-miles offer
