# Negotiation And Pre-Negotiation Playbook

Last updated: March 26, 2026

## Core rule

Negotiate the `out-the-door price`, not the monthly payment.

Monthly payment talk lets the dealer hide:

- a weak selling price
- long loan terms
- marked-up APR
- add-ons in finance

## The best pre-negotiation workflow

1. Pick one exact vehicle configuration.
2. Build a pricing baseline with Edmunds, KBB, and TrueCar.
3. Pull current incentives from CarsDirect, Edmunds, and TrueCar.
4. Contact multiple dealers by email or text.
5. Ask for a written out-the-door quote with a full line-item breakdown.
6. Tell each dealer you are buying within a short window and comparing written offers only.
7. Use the best written offer to move the others down.

## What to ask every dealer

Send this:

```text
I am ready to buy a new [year make model trim] in [color/options if important] within the next [48-72 hours / this week].

Please send your best written out-the-door price, including:
- selling price before incentives
- all manufacturer rebates/incentives applied and listed separately
- dealer discount listed separately
- doc fee
- add-ons or accessories
- tax, title, registration
- VIN or stock number

I am contacting multiple dealers and will move forward based on the best complete out-the-door offer, not monthly payment.

If you have a lower price contingent on financing, trade-in, loyalty, conquest, student, military, or membership eligibility, list that separately.
```

## The best negotiation sequence

1. Lock the vehicle.
2. Lock the selling price before financing.
3. Apply incentives.
4. Review fees and add-ons.
5. Review financing.
6. Review trade-in separately.

Never let the dealer blend those into one payment-based conversation.

## Target-setting rules

### Use invoice as a floor reference, not the goal

Edmunds and KBB both make the same basic point: invoice is not actual dealer net cost once holdback and dealer-side incentives enter the picture.

Practical implication:

- If market pricing is strong and inventory is tight, your realistic target may be near MSRP even if invoice is much lower.
- If incentives are rich and inventory is aging, your target can be below invoice.

### Use market price to decide whether the quote is real

Use TrueCar and KBB for this question:

`What are comparable buyers actually paying in my region right now?`

That matters more than the dealer's claimed discount from MSRP.

### Use current incentives to decide structure

Use CarsDirect and TrueCar for this question:

`Is cash rebate, subsidized APR, or a lease support program the better deal?`

This changes month to month and often cannot be solved from MSRP alone.

## The biggest negotiation traps

### Trap: "You're below MSRP, so this is already a great deal."

Response:

```text
I’m comparing against current market pricing and current incentives, not just MSRP. Please send your best out-the-door number.
```

### Trap: "This rebate requires our financing."

Response:

```text
Please show me the full terms, including APR, term, lender, and whether that pricing is still competitive if I use outside financing.
```

### Trap: "Let's talk monthly payment."

Response:

```text
I will review financing after we agree on the vehicle price and full out-the-door amount.
```

### Trap: "These accessories are already on the car."

Response:

```text
Please break out every dealer-installed item and its price. If those items are not optional, I need your best out-the-door number including them.
```

### Trap: "This is the price if you qualify for every incentive."

Response:

```text
Please separate guaranteed discounts from conditional incentives and list each eligibility requirement.
```

## When membership programs are smart

Use Costco or AAA when:

- you want a lower-friction process
- you dislike negotiation
- the market for the model is normal, not unusually hot

Still verify the offer with Edmunds, KBB, and TrueCar. Prearranged pricing is often good, but not always best-in-market.

## When to use an aggressive dealer-competition strategy

Use direct email / text competition when:

- inventory is plentiful
- the model has rebates or special APR
- you can contact at least 4 dealers
- month-end or quarter-end timing gives dealers more motivation

## Timing rules that usually help

- End of month helps when dealers are chasing targets.
- End of quarter can be stronger.
- End of model year helps most on outgoing inventory.
- Rich incentive months matter more than generic holiday advertising.

## Finance-office rules

After you agree on price:

- Decline to decide immediately on extras.
- Get GAP, warranties, service contracts, tire-and-wheel, and maintenance quoted separately.
- Compare outside financing before accepting dealer APR.
- Read the final buyer's order line by line.

## A practical target framework

Use this order:

1. `Market target`: the best estimate of a fair local selling price from Edmunds, KBB, and TrueCar.
2. `Incentive target`: subtract only the incentives you definitely qualify for.
3. `Fee-adjusted target`: add unavoidable tax/title/registration and a reasonable doc fee.
4. `Walk-away number`: the highest total out-the-door price you will accept.

If the dealer cannot beat your walk-away number in writing, move on.
